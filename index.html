About Amber Wilkinson

This land runs in the family.
The science is hers.

Her grandfather was a Georgia State Forester. Her family has worked this land for generations. Land stewardship isn't a talking point for Amber Wilkinson, it's what she grew up watching.

She's spent time learning the forestry side of the operation alongside the land manager who helps her family manage their timber, which is where the idea for site prep spraying took root. She saw how overspraying could kill the oak trees a landowner wanted to save, and thought: there has to be a better way.

Add a marine science degree, a commercial drone license, FAA Part 137 certification, and a Georgia Pesticide Contractor license — and you've got someone who can look at your field, your crops, maintain the cleanlieness of your lakes and streams, knock out your pest problem, and tell you exactly what application gives you the best outcome for your specific land. Not just what'll kill the bug, but what's best for your specific case. Every time.
